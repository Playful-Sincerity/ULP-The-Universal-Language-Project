# Dimensional Recursion — Where Does It Stop?

## The Expanded Ladder
Building on the 13-tier dimensional ladder. A group of societies is a new dimension — like sentience or something. Then you have a group of those. You have a consciousness, and in order to have multiple of those, you need a society. To have multiple societies, you need the dimension above — the social dynamic where the only thing you can see is the other groups around you.

Then you need a plane of it — to see many groups. Then to group that and see it again.

## The Recursion Question
Where does it become the universe as a whole? At what number are you simulating the dimension of the final one, where there's nothing else? How do you stop the recursion?

This is a critical theoretical question: does the dimensional ladder have a fixed terminus, or does it recurse infinitely? If it terminates, what makes the final dimension final? If it recurses, how is the universe a finite structure?

## 0-Dimensionalization
Each higher dimension lets you compress/encode the dimension below — you "0-dimensionalize" lower dimensions to create a space where multiple can interact. The dimension above is always the host for multiplicity at the dimension below.
