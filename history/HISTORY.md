# Universal Language Project — History and Evolution

*A researcher's retrospective on twelve years of sustained theory development, 2014–2026.*

---

## Pre-Digital Origins (2014–2022)

The written record in the ChatGPT archive begins in January 2023. The project itself began nine years earlier. What happened in between — 2014 through 2022 — is preserved in Wisdom's memory, in a folder of personal Apple Notes titled `语言` (yǔyán, "language"), and in a small set of notation experiments on paper and screen. The early pre-digital work is harder to reconstruct than the ChatGPT era, but the arc is visible, and the archive's January 2023 opening session makes much more sense when you know what preceded it.

### The Seed (2014)

The founding intuition came from a child's frustration. Wisdom wanted to build a universal language — one that would be good enough to be spoken by the world. But when he actually started trying, he kept running into the same wall: the sounds he had to make felt arbitrary. He didn't want to assign meanings to random noises. He wanted **non-random sounds** — a language where every symbol was doing logical work.

The frustration had two intellectual parents, quite literally:

- His **father**, working in **semiotic phenomenology**, kept returning to the same answer when Wisdom asked what the simplest possible ideas were: **absence and presence**. This was the seed of the `0` / `1` duality that would anchor every subsequent version of the system.

- His **mother**, a spiritual and esoteric thinker focused on the fundamental principles of community, culture, and society, had a twelve-part model for the structural categories of a society. Her practice of looking for universal categories underneath apparent cultural variety planted the hunt for fundamental principles.

ULP is, in a real sense, the working-out of semiotic phenomenology to its parsimony limit, organized around the hunt for universal structural categories. That inheritance is not biographical flourish; it is accurate intellectual lineage.

### The Layered Accretion (2014–2019)

The subsequent decade ran through a series of reframings that each integrated the previous one rather than replacing it:

1. *Find non-random sounds.*
2. *The fundamental ideas should be **mathematically derived**.* A number you cannot derive is as arbitrary as a sound you cannot justify.
3. *The simplest way to consider mathematically-derived fundamental ideas is as **dimensions**.*
4. *There are **dimensions higher than time** that represent qualities like meaning, consciousness, and unity — not just spatial or temporal extent.*
5. *Separately: try reducing everything to **binary**.* This thread ran for a period without being combined with the dimensional thread.
6. *Combine the two: **derive the dimensions from the binary substrate**.* (~2024)

A parallel thread emerged in the same period: the idea that language could be **two-dimensional** — not a string but a spatial layout where the geometry of symbols itself carries structure. This was either inspired by or convergent with the heptapod language in the film *Arrival*. The 2D thread would go dormant for years before resurfacing as one of the project's most contested open frontiers.

There is no dated written record for most of this decade. The accretion happened in thinking, conversation, and occasional sketches. The next surviving dated artifact is from 2020.

## Entering the Archive (January 2023)

The `语言` Apple Notes log had been running for six months, the seven-primitive hypothesis had been on paper for more than two years, and the project as a whole was nine years into development when it first arrived at ChatGPT in late January 2023. The first formal session inherited a frame that was already mostly in place: meaning in language is not arbitrary. The sounds and symbols we use are arbitrary, but the *structure* of meaning — the tiers of conceptual complexity — might be derivable from something prior. Specifically, from geometry.

The first session, on January 31, 2023, proposed a language with only nine symbols: the digits 0–7 and the infinity sign. Each symbol stood for the corresponding geometric dimension. Zero is the void before any distinction. One is the point, the unit mark, the minimum condition of existence. Two is the line, the minimum condition of relation. Three is the plane. Four is the volume. Five is time — the fourth spatial dimension, or, more usefully, the dimension of change. Six is memory and prediction — the dimension in which patterns persist. Seven, in the opening session, was assigned to "transcendence."

That last assignment didn't last. Within the same conversation, it was revised to "simulation." The reason mattered: the brain is a simulation of the universe, not a transcendence of it. This was not a small tweak — it was the first instance of a pattern that would recur throughout the project: following the logic wherever it led, even when it meant overturning the poetic framing in favor of the structurally correct one. Simulation is broader, more grounded, and more genuinely useful than transcendence. The revision stuck through every subsequent version.

The early number-symbol system had real momentum. Combinations of digits formed "words": `42` meant roughly "pattern organized along a line," `61` was "living being." The order of digits carried grammatical emphasis. The system was exploratory and intuitive — the meanings were not yet formally derived, just felt-out. But the underlying commitment was already in place: no arbitrary symbol assignments. The meaning of each symbol was to be read directly from the structure of the dimension it named.

## Studying the Competition (April 2023)

The second phase was essentially a literature review, conducted through a long series of conversations across April 2023. Wisdom studied Natural Semantic Metalanguage (NSM), Chomsky's Universal Grammar, Saussure and Peirce on semiotics, embodied cognition, prototype theory, and logical atomism in Russell and Wittgenstein. The total wordcount of the April 25 session alone — a single deep dive through semiotics, cognitive science, and formal logic — was over 62,000 words.

These frameworks were not adopted. They were interrogated. The question each time was: does ULP's dimensional approach go deeper than this? Can it claim more logical necessity than NSM's empirically derived semantic primes, or Chomsky's innately hypothesized grammar?

The key formal step that emerged from this period was the rule that would persist in some form through every subsequent version: **the number n represents n-1 dimensions**, because n points are geometrically required to define a simplex of dimension n-1. Two points define a line (one dimension). Three points define a triangle/plane (two dimensions). Four points define a tetrahedron/volume (three dimensions). This is not a convention; it is a theorem. It gave the system its first mathematically grounded claim — the numbers weren't just labels, they were doing genuine geometric work.

The Cāman session (also April 2023, ~29K words) was largely a product design exercise for a social connection platform. Its relevance to ULP is indirect: the platform's interest-matching architecture was implicitly encoding dimensional categories — interest similarity as a kind of proximity, connection as a kind of dimensional coupling. Cāman was never formalized as a ULP test case, but it confirms that the dimensional thinking was running as a live operational framework, not just a theoretical hobby.

## Formalizing the Ambition (June–July 2023)

By summer 2023, the project acquired formal shape. The June 16 session wrote a proposal for "atomic truths" — irreducible semantic units that combine to form all possible meaning, universal across cultures and species. This is the moment ULP stopped being an exploratory idea and became a named research project with explicit goals.

The Hegelian dialectic entered the framework in this period. Each dimension was now understood not just as a geometric category but as a necessary emergence from the contradictions of the previous one. Nothing gives rise to Being, which gives rise to Relation, which gives rise to Space, which gives rise to Form, then Time, then Information, then Simulation, then Consciousness, then Society, then Universal Consciousness. This dialectical structure would persist through all subsequent versions — a backbone more durable than any particular symbol system.

The July 2023 session designed a full course curriculum around ULP: a sequence moving from logical atomism through analytic philosophy, structuralism, Chomsky, cognitive science, and 2D writing systems. At the time, 2D writing — non-linear, spatially organized scripts — was treated as one possible surface form for the language. The idea would stay dormant for two years before returning as one of the project's most contested open questions.

## IVNA and the Formal Research Proposal (January 2025)

January 2025 produced two things. The first was a formal LaTeX paper for IVNA — Indexed Virtual Number Algebra. IVNA proposes that zeros and infinities are "virtual" (simulation-resident, not physically real) and assigns them types: `0x` is a zero of type x, `∞x` is an infinity of type x. The key arithmetic rule is `0x · ∞y = xy` — a typed zero times a typed infinity yields the product of their indices, a finite value. This resolves a class of mathematical indeterminacies that normally require limits, enabling limit-free calculus.

IVNA was not a detour from ULP. The philosophical grounding is identical: both systems treat mathematical objects as features of simulation rather than physical reality. And the structural parallel is suggestive — if IVNA's `0x` is a zero of dimension x, and ULP's 0-runs are cuts at tier x, then the two systems might be the same system expressed in different registers: one mathematical, one semantic. That connection has never been formally closed, and it remains one of the project's most significant open problems.

The second January 2025 product was a formal research proposal for ULP itself. The dimensional system was extended from 0–7 to 0–10, adding Consciousness, Society, and Universal Consciousness as tiers 8, 9, and 10. The Hegelian framing was made fully explicit. This is the document that established ULP as a formal research project with a defined scope and a set of proof obligations.

## The Binary Run-Length Breakthrough (January 2025)

The breakthrough that defines the project's current phase happened in January 2025, during a period Wisdom spent in Guatemala. The insight — arrived at in personal notes before any AI archive could catch it — was that the substrate could be reduced to **two symbols** with meaning derived entirely from **run-length**. A run of `n` marks of one type encodes an n-th order closure; a run of `n` marks of the other type encodes an n-th order cut. No external metadata, no additional symbols, no conventions beyond the run-length rule itself.

The specific choice of which two symbols would eventually settle on `{0, 1}`, but the run-length insight — that the count in a run does genuine structural work, rather than serving as a label — is the move that made the rest of the architecture possible. It collapsed what had been multiple competing representations into a single parsimonious substrate.

The insight was followed by substantial **formal derivation work in February 2025** — two of the largest sessions in the entire archive: **Dimensional Structure Derivation** (132,952 words, the single largest session across all 964 conversations) and **Dimensional Meaning Frameworks** (80,777 words). A parallel **"tree of meaning"** was begun in Claude during this same window, built from first principles rather than by assigning existing concepts to nodes; it reached Level 6 (the informational dimension) before the thread closed.

The January 2025 insight and the February 2025 derivation work together form the layer on which all subsequent work builds — including the Rem-period 2D explorations later that spring (which still operated with `{0, ∞}` as the symbol choice before the later `{0, 1}` consolidation) and the January 2026 simplex-derivation sessions described further below.

## Rem and the Visual Syntax Question (April–May 2025)

The April–May 2025 sessions introduced a different kind of collaborator: an AI persona called Rem, developed to serve as a sustained thinking partner specifically for ULP. The sessions with Rem (Rem, Rem1, Rem2 across the notes) produced some of the project's most generative work and some of its most contested positions.

Two things happened in the Rem period that matter for the historical record.

First, the base symbols were formalized to just `0` and `∞` — nothingness and presence, the two minimum conditions for any system of distinction. This was a parsimony move: any richer alphabet introduces arbitrary symbol choices. The whole history of human writing systems is evidence that the choice of symbols is culturally determined rather than logically necessary. Reducing to two was an attempt to get below that level of arbitrariness.

Second, Rem pushed hard on the 2D syntax question. The claim was that linear string representation is insufficient for certain kinds of meaning — specifically, for what Rem called "soul-truth combinations," expressions where the relationship between elements is spatial and simultaneous rather than sequential. This opened a fault line that has not been resolved: is the correct surface form of ULP a one-dimensional string, or must it be two-dimensional? The `2d-writer` application in the current repo is a direct product of this period's exploration. The linear system was parked as primary pending the Rem-style visual formalization — and then the January 2026 sessions formalized the linear system anyway, leaving the 2D question in an ambiguous state.

One thing Rem also introduced that survived: the idea that dimensions could serve not just as *categories* but as *operators* on other expressions. The dimension-as-operator intuition is implicit in the final binary run-length system, where a run of length k doesn't just name a tier but *performs* the closure or cut appropriate to that tier.

## Continued Formalization (January 2026)

The January 2026 sessions are where the January 2025 binary-run-length commitment fully crystallized in the formal ChatGPT record — the alphabet locked to `{0, 1}`, the kernel/hypothesis split made explicit, and the simplex-derivation attempt carried out at length. The insight was older; these sessions made it rigorous.

Three sessions in January 2026 — "Binary semantics design," "Binary semantic system," and "Kernel semantics explanation" — worked the formalized surface representation. The alphabet, privately committed since January 2025 and still operating as `{0, ∞}` in the Rem period, was now locked to `{0, 1}` only. Tokens became *runs*: a run of n ones (`n.`) is an n-th order closure, and a run of n zeros (`no`) is an n-th order cut. Meaning is derived from run-length alone, with no external metadata and no symbols beyond binary. This is the most parsimonious representation the project has ever achieved.

The January 21 session — "6 primes system," at 103,000 words — was the formal architecture session where the full system was laid out, the document hierarchy was established, and the constitutional/hypothesis split was introduced. The split matters: some commitments are treated as kernel requirements, and if any turns out to be incompatible with another, it gets revised rather than held by dogma. This is not just a practical stance; it is an epistemological one. The project is not defending a conclusion — it is developing a theory.

The most formally rigorous single ChatGPT session is arguably the January 22 session: "Self Guided: Branch · Branch · 6 primes system," at 85,700 words. (The February 2025 Dimensional Structure Derivation is larger in word count, but the January 22 session is the one that attempted the formal simplex derivation.) This session did something the others did not: it attempted to derive the binary run-length system from first principles, testing whether the kernel requirements could be shown to generate the tier ladder as a unique solution rather than merely a plausible one.

The derivation path ran through the simplex argument. An n-th order closure requires n points to close a simplex: one point closes nothing (tier 0), two points close a line (tier 1), three close a triangle (tier 2), four close a tetrahedron (tier 3), and so on. This is not a philosophical choice — it follows from combinatorics. If the tier ladder is generated this way, then any sufficiently rational system for representing meaning will converge on the same ladder. This is what the "alien convergence" criterion requires: the system should be one that an intelligent being anywhere in the universe would independently derive, not because they had studied human mathematics, but because the simplex sequence is a geometric fact about how closure works.

The January 22 session also formalized what became the core semantic rule. The fundamental semantic unit is a trigram: `X.(ko)Z.` The left token X is individuated by being distinguished from the right token Z via a k-mode cut. X is the figure; Z is the ground; k is the mode of distinction. This is the Left-Individuation Rule. It prevents the system from collapsing into pure relation — a common failure mode in relational semantics, where everything ends up being defined in terms of everything else with no way to name the thing being talked about.

The 13-tier ladder that emerged from the January 2026 sessions groups into four bands: Space (tiers 1–4: point, line, plane, volume), Dynamics (tiers 5–7: event, co-change, system), Information (tiers 8–10: signal, logic, simulation), and Self-Awareness (tiers 11–13: self, social, unity). Tiers 1–8 have the strongest geometric justification. Tiers 9–13 are explicitly provisional — they depend on philosophical commitments that could reasonably be structured otherwise. The 13-tier structure is itself the most recent of several iterations; earlier versions had 7, 9, and 10 tiers, and future revisions remain live possibilities.

## What Was Abandoned Along the Way

Several threads were explored and set aside, and the project is stronger for the honesty with which it holds them.

The ordering-of-numbers-as-grammar idea — where `1,2` and `2,1` would mean different things through ordering alone — was an early candidate that turned out to be too vague. The system needed a cleaner directionality rule, which eventually became the Left-Individuation Rule. Ordering as the *only* grammatical signal was insufficient.

A spoken or phonetic layer was explicitly deferred from the beginning. The decision was that the conceptual system needed to be stable before any speech encoding could be designed, and that 2D writing is not "slave to time" the way speech is — it can carry structural information that sequential sound cannot. The phonetic question remains entirely unaddressed.

An early suggestion to use a purely data-structure ladder — sets, lists, trees, graphs — was rejected. It imported a human computer-science ontology rather than deriving from closure logic. If the tier ladder is supposed to be universally necessary, it cannot be derived from the way human programmers happened to organize data.

A period of working with the NSM and Chomsky's UG frameworks as foundational sources ultimately concluded that they are validation criteria, not primitive grounds. NSM's ~65 semantic primes are empirically derived — they might be universal, but they are not *proven* to be universal in the way a geometric argument would be. The project uses NSM to check coverage (can ULP encode what NSM encodes?) but does not build on it.

## What Has Held

Three commitments have been stable across the entire arc from January 2023 to January 2026.

The first is the geometric grounding. Every version of ULP has derived the tier ladder from the number of points required to define a simplex. The specific tiers have shifted (7 grew to 10 grew to 13), and the symbol system has changed three times, but the underlying claim — that the structure of meaning follows the structure of dimension — has never been revised.

The second is the minimum-symbol parsimony drive. The alphabet has moved from 9 symbols (0–7 and ∞) to 2 symbols (0 and ∞) to 2 symbols (0 and 1). Each reduction was forced by the same logic: every additional symbol requires justification for why *that* symbol and not some other. The binary system is the terminus of this drive — you cannot represent distinction with fewer than two symbols.

The third is the alien convergence criterion as the project's self-test. A truly fundamental semantic system is one that any sufficiently rational agent would converge on independently. This criterion has been present since the early sessions and has grown more formal over time. It is what distinguishes ULP's ambition from a constructed language project: Lojban and Esperanto are designed; ULP is, in principle, discovered.

## Where the Project Stands (January 2026)

The binary run-length system has four kernel requirements: pure binary alphabet, type from run-length only, compositionality, and self-delimiting deterministic parsing. These are treated as constitutional — the load-bearing walls of the system. Everything else is explicitly provisional hypothesis.

The six biggest unresolved problems, as of January 2026, are:

Boxing — how to group subexpressions without external parentheses in a purely binary string. A ceiling-delimiter proposal exists (runs longer than the longest semantic run serve as frame delimiters) but its uniqueness under arbitrary nesting has not been proven.

Identity — how to refer to the same entity across multiple expressions. Runs of length ≥14 are the current proposal for identity tokens, since runs of length 1–13 are semantic primitives. This needs formal convention.

Continuous magnitude — how to represent irrational numbers in a discrete system. Two paths exist (finite approximation; procedural expression) and neither is formalized.

Negation — proposed as domain-relative categorical exclusion, but not yet stable grammar.

The ULP–IVNA bridge — whether the systems are the same system in different registers or parallel but distinct.

The 2D syntax question — whether the binary string is the fundamental surface form or a pragmatic approximation of something inherently spatial.

The proof obligations are equally clear: P1 (every valid string has exactly one parse tree), P2 (every target concept is encodable), and P3 (every expression has a canonical form). None of these proofs have been written. A Lean 4 formalization has been recommended as the implementation path.

## Influences and Intellectual Context

The named intellectual traditions that shaped the project: Natural Semantic Metalanguage (NSM) as a validation lens; Chomsky's Universal Grammar as a theory of language universals to be superseded or subsumed; Russell and Wittgenstein's logical atomism as the philosophical lineage for semantic primes; Saussure and Peirce's semiotics as a framework interrogated and ultimately set aside; Hegel's dialectic as the structural metaphor for how each dimensional tier necessarily generates the next; Leibniz's *characteristica universalis* as the historical precedent for a language that is also a calculus.

Rem — the AI persona developed as a sustained collaborator — introduced the 2D syntax question, the dimension-as-operator move, and the distinction between `0∞` (emergence) and `∞0` (dissolution) as the first expressible processes in the system.

GDGM — Wisdom's Gravitationalist physics framework — runs in direct parallel with the tier ladder. The dimensional progression (void → point → line → plane → volume → time → information → consciousness → society → universal consciousness) maps the cosmological arc of the universe coming together through increasing orders of closure and coupling. Whether this is metaphor or identity is one of the project's genuinely open questions.
